Where Elephants Live

African elephants inhabit savannahs, forests, deserts, and marshes across Africa. Asian elephants are found in forests, grasslands, and wetlands in South and Southeast Asia.

Elephants are highly adaptable and can travel long distances to find food and water. Habitat loss and fragmentation, however, threaten their populations.

Regions

  1. African Savannah and Forests
  2. Asian Forests and Grasslands
  3. Wetlands and Riverbanks

Conservation efforts aim to protect elephant habitats through national parks, wildlife corridors, and anti-poaching initiatives.

Habitat protection is critical for maintaining healthy elephant populations and biodiversity in their ecosystems.

"Protecting elephant habitats benefits countless other species sharing the same ecosystems." – WWF

Quick Tip

Elephants need large areas to roam; a herd may travel up to 50 km per day searching for food and water.
