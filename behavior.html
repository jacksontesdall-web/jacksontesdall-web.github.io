Social Structure

Elephants are highly social animals that live in herds led by an older matriarch. Herds typically consist of related females and their young. Males often leave the herd when they reach adolescence.

They communicate using vocalizations, body language, and even seismic signals. Trumpeting, rumbling, and low-frequency sounds allow them to convey messages over long distances.

Daily Activities

  • Feeding: They eat up to 150 kg of vegetation per day.
  • Bathing: Elephants enjoy mud and water to cool off.
  • Playing: Young elephants often engage in playful wrestling and trunk games.
  • Traveling: Herds may move several kilometers daily for food and water.
"Elephants show empathy, cooperation, and grief—remarkable for any non-human species." – Smithsonian Magazine

Studies have shown that elephants remember locations of water sources, other elephants, and even humans that have posed threats.

They also exhibit mourning behavior, often gathering around the bones of deceased elephants and touching them with their trunks.

Fun Fact

Elephants can recognize themselves in mirrors, a sign of high intelligence.
