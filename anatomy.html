Physical Features

Elephants have unique anatomical features, including their long trunks, tusks, large ears, and massive legs. Each feature serves important functions for survival in the wild.

The trunk is a fusion of the nose and upper lip, highly flexible, and contains over 40,000 muscles. It is used for breathing, drinking, grabbing food, and social interactions.

Tusks are modified incisors made of ivory. They help elephants dig for water, strip bark from trees, and can be used as weapons for defense.

Other Body Parts

  • Ears: Used for thermoregulation and communication.
  • Legs: Strong and pillar-like to support massive weight.
  • Skin: Thick and wrinkled to protect from sun and parasites.
  • Tail: Used to swat insects and communicate.
"The elephant's body is perfectly adapted for survival in its environment." – National Geographic
